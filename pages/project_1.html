Project 1 - Narrative Interactive Story

Figure 1: Character Sheet of Oscar the Cat. By Sahil Sran, 2022.

Synopsis

Cat and Mouse: Unbounded Rivalry was a final group project as a part of the Narrative and New Media class in SFU's Interactive Arts and Technology (IAT) program. Our team's goal was to develop a narrative interactive story that featured multiple divergent paths that alter the outcome of the story. Our team used Figma, the online interaction prototyping software, as it enabled us to simultaneously work on the project together. This story features the conflict between a cat and mouse who have to put their differences aside to escape an endless expanse of forest. As the most savvy artist in the group, I was assigned the largest role of drawing every character and background in the story. Our team initially thought of dividing the illustration work between two team members. However, we ultimately decided against it since assigning two artists would've created inconsistencies between art styles.

Storyboarding & Conceptualizing

The process began with the script, which would be categorized into scenes that I would draw. As Figures 2 and 3 illustrate, I started with a brief draft sketch to visualize the scene's environment, as well as the perspective and placement of characters. From there, I finished the drawing by cleaning up the line art, and blending colours to give the scenes more definition, as shown in Figures 4 and 6. Next, as evident in Figures 5 and 7, the artworks are added as panels in Figma with the respective characters and dialogue. On top of illustrating the backgrounds,I was tasked with drawing the various poses for each character, as shown in Figure 1.

Challenges & Resolutions

One of the biggest problems that I encountered immediately was the sheer volume of work. With approximately 20 scenes and several character drawings/ expressions, time management and consistency were of the utmost importance. Therefore, I was forced to spend less time on each drawing and compromise for low fidelity simply due to a lack of time. With this strategy, I was able to complete the minimum requirements for the story. Another potential solution would've been to have a2nd or 3rd artist. However, this would require the team to use drawing software that supports rigging to streamline the process of building every scene, in order to maintain consistency throughout the project. Due to the team's lack of experience and access to these sophisticated programs, it was ultimately my duty to draw all assets, for which I used Clip Studio Paint EX.

Experience Takeaways

From this experience, I learned that it is sometimes okay to assume a larger role in a team environment if it ultimately benefits the end product. Distributing work equally among teammates is taught in IAT as the most ideal outcome, but that sometimes proves impossible for the nature of projects, where one skill will inevitably outshine the rest. Alternatively, in a professional setting, teams would be specialized to ensure there is an adequate balance of skill sets to ensure each role within the project is fulfilled equally. Due to the nature of team formation within IAT, this simply wasn't the case and ultimately I proved to be the strongest link within the team, which led to completion of the project.
