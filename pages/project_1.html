Project 1 - Narrative Interactive Story

Figure 5: Character Sheet of Oscar the Cat. By Parampreet Sran, 2022

Cat and Mouse - Unbounded Rivalry

Cat and Mouse: Unbounded Rivalry was a final group project as a part of the Narrative and New Media class in SFU's Interactive Arts and Technology program. Our team's goal was to develop a narrative interactive story that featured diverting paths that alter the outcome of the story. Our team used Figma, the online interaction prototyping software as it enabled us to simultaneously work on the project together. This story features a cat and mouse who have to put their differences aside to escape an endless expanse of forest. As the most savvy artist in the group, I was assigned the largest role of drawing every panel in the story. Our team initially thought of dividing the illustration work between two team members. However, we ultimately decided against it since assigning two artists would've created inconsistencies between art styles.

The process began with the script, which would be categorized into scenes that I would draw. As Figures 6 and 7 illustrate, I start with a brief draft sketch to visualize the scene's environment, as well as the perspective and placement of characters. From there, I finish the drawing by cleaning up the line art, and blending colours to give the works more definition, as shown in Figures 8 and 9. Next, as evident in Figures 10 and 11, the artworks are added as panels in Figma with the respective characters and dialogue. On top of the backgrounds, I was tasked with drawing the various poses for each character, as shown in Figure 5.
