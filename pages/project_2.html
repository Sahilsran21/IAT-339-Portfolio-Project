Project 2 - Unity Game

Figure 1: Storyboard of various scenes/segments in Argon Chalice. By Sahil Sran, 2020.

Synopsis

Argon Chalice was a group project as a part of the Foundations of Game Design class in SFU’s Interactive Arts and Technology (IAT) program. The team’s goal was to develop a game that follows a standard narrative structure that features a collection of levels that increase in difficulty, followed by a final boss. The game is about Owen, an outdated robot that awakens to a war-torn dystopian future in search of a memory module that contains its memories. It proceeds through a tower where a patrol team is searching to destroy him. After facing and immobilizing its creator, Owen's past reveals that the world and people he once loved are both gone, replaced by a world of war between two organizations known as the World Union and Rouge Faction (Figure 1). The game ends on a split choice Owen makes on which organization he will join. My other 3 team members took up roles with project management, programming, and game designing, which left me in charge of art direction.

Storyboarding & Conceptualizing

As art director, this was a fun project to immerse myself into, as there were a few character designs I had in mind to portray the characters and environments. For instance, I wanted Owen and his starting environment to have an outdated look, which would contrast the more modern look of the rogue faction units. To express this contrast further, I gave Owen a warmer colour scheme and the Rogue Faction a colder palette (Figure 2). Additionally, I gave the boss a sinister look to express the superiority of the current world’s Rogue Faction over Owen’s line of robots (Figure 3). The team's original intention was to have the characters animated, but due to time constraints, we prioritized the game’s functionality over aesthetic details. This also led to me reusing certain assets for in-game cutscenes (Figure 4).

Figure 2: Concept Illustrations of the protagonist OWEN (left) and an NPC enemy patrol bot (right). By Sahil Sran, 2020.

Challenges & Resolutions

One challenge I came across was the fact that I was not as invested in working with Github and Unity for designing the game, which was the primary intention of the class. As the team’s graphics illustrator, I found myself so caught up in producing assets that I would simply upload assets to Google Drive, rather than passing the learning curve of working with Github and Unity. This step wasn’t essential since I was still able to produce the required assets for the game, but it would have certainly been to my benefit. The other difficulty was that the class was conducted virtually due to Covid-19. This made matters challenging when it came time to explain concepts and creative details, but thanks to the superb management of the team, these issues didn’t pose a serious issue.

Experience Takeaways

Contrary to my experience with project 1, I had a more positive experience illustrating for Argon Chalice since each team member had a role that bore equal weight as my art direction did. Therefore, the work was more equally distributed since the success of the project did not hinge on my ability to produce assets quickly. I have a positive reception of this project since every team member was passionate enough to fulfill their part. As stated before, I hope to become more involved in learning Unity and Github workflow in the future, since my role as illustrator prohibited me from doing so. Becoming sufficient in Github’s workflow will also become essential when it comes time to share programming-based projects on my portfolio, hence why I prioritize that skill moving forward.
